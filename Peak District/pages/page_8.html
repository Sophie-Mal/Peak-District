Visit The Peak District

Transport

Buses:

Bus routes to the Peak District provide convenient access to the region's stunning landscapes and attractions, catering to both visitors and locals alike.
These routes typically originate from nearby towns and cities, offering scenic journeys through picturesque countryside.
Passengers can relax and enjoy the views while traveling to popular destinations such as Bakewell, Buxton, and Castleton.
Additionally, some bus routes are designed specifically for sightseeing purposes, providing informative commentary along the way to enhance the experience.
With frequent services and well-connected routes, bus travel offers an eco-friendly and hassle-free way to explore the natural beauty of the Peak District without the need for a car.

Trains:

Train routes to the Peak District offer a convenient and scenic way to access this stunning region, connecting it with major cities and towns across the UK.
Passengers can embark on picturesque journeys through the rolling hills and charming villages, enjoying panoramic views of the countryside along the way.
Popular destinations such as Buxton, Matlock, and Hope Valley are easily accessible by train, making it an ideal option for day trips or weekend getaways.
Some train routes, like the Hope Valley Line, are renowned for their breathtaking scenery, providing passengers with an unforgettable travel experience.
With frequent services and comfortable amenities, train travel to the Peak District offers a relaxing and eco-friendly alternative to driving.
