Visit The Peak District

Visitor Attractions

Bakewell

Bakewell, located in the Peak District, Derbyshire, is a picturesque market town known for its scenic beauty and rich history.
Nestled along the River Wye, it features charming stone buildings, historic sites like All Saints' Church, and the famous Bakewell Pudding.
Its welcoming atmosphere is enhanced by independent shops, cafes, and traditional pubs.
Surrounded by stunning landscapes, Bakewell offers easy access to outdoor activities such as walking and cycling, with highlights including the Monsal Trail.
The town's blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and vibrant community makes it a delightful destination.

Ladybower Reservoir

Ladybower Reservoir, located in the Peak District, is a stunning Y-shaped reservoir renowned for its scenic beauty and tranquil environment.
Surrounded by rolling hills, lush woodlands, and heather-clad moorlands, it offers picturesque views and a peaceful atmosphere.
The reservoir is popular for walking, cycling, and fishing, with paths like the Ladybower Loop providing easy access to its scenic shores.
Notable features include the impressive dam structures and the 'plugholes' spillways.
Ladybower's combination of natural beauty, recreational opportunities, and historical significance makes it a charming and serene destination for visitors.

Castleton

Castleton, nestled in the heart of the Peak District, is a picturesque village celebrated for its charming atmosphere and rich history.
Surrounded by dramatic landscapes, including the iconic Mam Tor and the stunning Hope Valley, it offers breathtaking views and numerous hiking opportunities.
Castleton is famous for its show caves, such as Peak Cavern and Blue John Cavern, where the rare Blue John stone is found.
The village's quaint streets are lined with traditional pubs, shops, and tea rooms.
Its unique blend of natural wonders, historical attractions, and welcoming community makes Castleton a delightful destination for visitors.
