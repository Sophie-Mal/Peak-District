Visit The Peak District

Activities

Rock Climbing

Rock climbing in the Peak District is renowned for its diverse and challenging routes, making it a premier destination for climbers of all levels.
The region boasts a variety of climbing experiences, from the gritstone edges of Stanage and Froggatt to the limestone cliffs of Raven Tor and Chee Dale.
Climbers are drawn to the Peak District for its scenic beauty, including dramatic crags, rolling hills, and panoramic views.
Popular climbing spots like Stanage Edge offer a range of routes, from beginner-friendly climbs to advanced challenges.
The combination of high-quality rock, stunning landscapes, and a supportive climbing community makes the Peak District an exceptional and inspiring location for rock climbing enthusiasts.

New Mills Walkway

The New Mills Walkway offers a scenic and leisurely stroll through the charming town of New Mills in the Peak District.
This picturesque path follows the route of the former railway line, providing a peaceful escape amid beautiful countryside scenery.
The walkway is suitable for all ages and abilities, making it an ideal option for a relaxing outing with family or friends.
Along the way, walkers can enjoy views of the River Goyt and the historic industrial architecture that characterizes the area.
With benches for resting and picnic spots for enjoying a snack, the New Mills Walkway offers a delightful opportunity to explore the natural and cultural heritage of the Peak District.

Sensory Walks

Sensory walks in the Peak District offer a unique and immersive experience for visitors to engage with nature using all their senses.
These walks are designed to awaken the senses, allowing participants to connect with the environment on a deeper level.
Along the trail, walkers may encounter various touchpoints, such as textured surfaces, aromatic plants, and intriguing sounds of nature.
The routes often lead through diverse landscapes, including woodlands, meadows, and streams, providing a rich tapestry of sensory stimuli.
Whether guided or self-led, sensory walks offer an opportunity to slow down, appreciate the beauty of the natural world, and foster a deeper connection with the environment in the tranquil surroundings of the Peak District.
